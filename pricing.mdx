## Call Pricing

Vapi charges a platform fee of **$0.05 per call minute**. Call costs are prorated down to the second so you don't have to worry about short calls (\<1 minute) getting rounded up.

You only pay for call time you actually use.

### Starter Credits

Every new account is granted **$10 in free credits** for testing & debugging. You can [start using Vapi](/quickstart/dashboard) without a credit card.

<Info>
  You can view your account's billing details at
  [dashboard.vapi.ai/billing](https://dashboard.vapi.ai/billing). There you can see daily usage,
  monthly usage, set usage limits, & more.
</Info>

### Exceeding Billing Limits

Once you have used all of your starter credits, or exceeded your set monthly usage limit, you will start seeing errors in your dashboard & via the API mentioning `Billing Limits Exceeded`.

### STT, LLM, TTS Provider Cost

During calls, API requests will be made to providers for Speech-to-text (STT), Text-to-speech (TTS), & Large Language Model (LLM) inference. There are 2 places these charges can end up:

1. **Provider-side:** in the account you have with the provider.
2. **With Vapi:** in your Vapi account.

#### Provider-side Billing

If we have [provider keys](/provider-keys) on file for a provider, the cost will be seen directly
in your account with the provider (Vapi will have made the API call to them with your secret
key).

No charge will be made to your Vapi account.

#### Billed with Vapi

If no key is found on-file for the provider, Vapi will make the API request itself (with Vapi's own keys, at Vapi's expense). This expense is then passed on [**at-cost**](/glossary#at-cost) to be billed directly to your Vapi account.

No charge will show up provider-side.

<Note>
  **A Note On Call Platform:** Whether you are making phone calls, or web calls, these pieces of the
  voice pipeline will always execute & generate costs.
</Note>

### Phone Calling Cost

During a **phone call**, cost will be incurred with programmable voice providers ([Twilio](https://www.twilio.com) & [Vonage](https://www.vonage.com)) per-minute (a cost completely separate from provisioning & owning the phone numbers themselves).

<Info>
  You can learn more about Twilio's per-minute calling fees
  [here](https://www.twilio.com/en-us/voice/pricing/us), & Vonage's per-minute fees
  [here](https://www.vonage.com/communications-apis/voice/pricing/).
</Info>

#### Imported Phone Numbers

Per-minute inbound/outbound phone call fees will be billed **directly with the respective provider** that you provisioned the phone number from (currently either Twilio or Vonage).

Your number lives with them, & you will be billed per-minute for calls inbound or outbound from those phone numbers with them.

No charge will be made to your Vapi account.

#### Phone Numbers Registered Through Vapi

When you purchase a phone number through Vapi, Vapi will provision the phone number (on your behalf) with Twilio.

Your **Vapi account** will be charged at-cost according to [Twilio's per-minute phone call pricing](https://www.twilio.com/en-us/voice/pricing/us).

## The Main Idea

The above may be a lot to absorb, but the main idea is that everything is designed to "just work". Whether you are billed provider-side, or on Vapi's side, you will never be charged with any margin for fees incurred during calls.

All calling costs would have been incurred anyway.
